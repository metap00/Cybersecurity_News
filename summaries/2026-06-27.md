### [Operation Endgame Dismantles Amadey and StealC Malware Infrastructure, Recovers 27M Stolen Credentials](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/amadey-and-stealc-malware-network.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News | **Published:** June 24–27, 2026

A sweeping international law enforcement operation — the latest chapter of Operation Endgame — has dismantled the criminal infrastructure powering two of the most prolific infostealer malware families: Amadey and StealC. Conducted between June 15 and 19, the action resulted in the seizure of 326 servers and 142 domains, recovery of 27 million stolen credentials, and the restriction of cryptocurrency assets worth more than $47 million linked to criminal proceeds.

Amadey served as an access broker, quietly gaining footholds on victim machines before deploying StealC to harvest passwords, browser data, and sensitive information. The two tools were collectively linked to more than 140,000 infected computers in the first two weeks of May 2026 alone, and were routinely used as a launchpad for ransomware campaigns and financial fraud targeting critical infrastructure.

The operation was coordinated across agencies from Canada, Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands, the UK, and the US, with substantial private-sector support from Microsoft, Bitdefender, ESET, Bitsight, IBM X-Force, Proofpoint, Infoblox, Shadowserver, and Orange Cyberdefense, among others. Authorities have identified and are pursuing suspects responsible for operating the networks.

Organizations should audit endpoints for Amadey/StealC indicators of compromise, rotate any credentials that may have been exposed, and review access logs for unusual authentication activity dating back to May 2026.

### [Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Zero-Day CVE-2026-20245 Exploited Months Before Disclosure](https://securityaffairs.com/194200/hacking/cisco-catalyst-sd-wan-zero-day-cve-2026-20245-exploited-months-before-disclosure.html)
**Source:** Security Affairs / SecurityWeek | **Published:** June 5–27, 2026

A high-severity command injection zero-day in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager (CVE-2026-20245, CVSS 7.8) was actively exploited by an unknown threat actor for at least two months before Cisco disclosed the flaw on June 5, 2026. The vulnerability exists in the CLI due to insufficient validation of user-supplied input and allows an authenticated attacker with netadmin privileges to upload a crafted file and execute arbitrary commands as root.

In at least one confirmed incident against a service provider, the attacker gained root access on SD-WAN management infrastructure, created a rogue privileged account named "troot," pushed unauthorized configuration changes to edge devices, and then ran anti-forensic scripts to erase evidence of the intrusion. Google Mandiant reported the vulnerability to Cisco after observing in-the-wild exploitation. Cisco ranks this as the seventh SD-WAN zero-day exploited in 2026.

The flaw affects all Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN deployment types including on-premises, Cloud-Pro, Cisco Managed Cloud, and FedRAMP environments. Fixed versions are available: 20.9.9.2, 20.12.7.2, 20.15.4.5, 20.15.5.3, 20.18.3.1, 26.1.1.2, and later. Organizations should apply patches immediately, audit for the "troot" account or other unauthorized accounts, and review configuration change logs for SD-WAN edge devices.

### [Tata Electronics Ransomware Breach Exposes Apple and Tesla Trade Secrets](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/tata-electronics-a-major-tech-supplier-to-apple-and-tesla-confirms-data-breach/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / CyberNews | **Published:** June 22–23, 2026

India's Tata Electronics — the primary manufacturer of Apple iPhones in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka — has confirmed a cybersecurity incident after the World Leaks ransomware group published over 200,000 files (630 GB) allegedly stolen from the company. The leaked cache reportedly contains Apple and Tesla mechanical and technical drawings, schematics, manufacturing specifications, employee passport scans, and internal documents spanning a decade of records.

World Leaks, believed to be a rebrand of the Hunter's International ransomware cartel, first listed Tata Electronics on its dark web leak site on June 12. Apple stated it is investigating the incident; Tata Electronics confirmed the breach but said operations were not impacted. The incident underscores the supply chain security risks facing major technology brands — a compromise of a Tier-1 hardware supplier can expose proprietary intellectual property belonging to multiple downstream customers simultaneously.

Organizations that work with Apple or Tesla as suppliers should review third-party access controls, audit what proprietary data is shared with manufacturing partners, and verify that those partners maintain adequate segmentation between production systems and administrative networks where sensitive documents are stored.

### [Check Point VPN Zero-Day CVE-2026-50751 Exploited by Qilin Ransomware — CISA Issues Emergency Directive](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-orders-feds-to-patch-check-point-flaw-exploited-by-ransomware-gangs/)
**Source:** BleepingComputer / Rapid7 | **Published:** June 8–11, 2026

A critical authentication bypass vulnerability in Check Point Remote Access VPN and Mobile Access (CVE-2026-50751, CVSS 9.3) is being actively exploited by affiliates of the Qilin ransomware group. The flaw exploits a logic error in certificate validation for configurations using the deprecated IKEv1 key exchange protocol, allowing an unauthenticated attacker to establish a valid VPN session without providing a legitimate password.

Exploitation was first observed as far back as May 7, 2026, with activity increasing sharply in early June. CISA added the vulnerability to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 8 and issued its fastest remediation window of 2026 — just three days — ordering Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies to apply patches by June 11. The directive was accompanied by guidance urging all private sector organizations to patch immediately as well.

Check Point has released a hotfix for affected products. Organizations running Remote Access VPN or Mobile Access with IKEv1 configured should apply the available security update immediately, audit VPN authentication logs for anomalous sessions from May 7 onward, and consider migrating to IKEv2 or other supported key exchange protocols.

### [Miasma Worm Returns: npm Supply Chain Attack Spreads via Phantom Gyp Technique](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/miasma-malware-targets-npm-packages-and.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News / Microsoft Security Blog | **Published:** June 2–27, 2026

The Miasma malware family — a self-spreading supply chain worm — has claimed a second wave of victims. After initially compromising 32 npm packages under the `@redhat-cloud-services` namespace on June 1 by hijacking Red Hat's CI/CD pipeline, attackers published poisoned versions of 20 additional packages in the Leo Platform npm ecosystem on June 24, 2026, in a coordinated burst spanning less than three seconds.

The malware employs a novel "Phantom Gyp" technique that abuses a 157-byte `binding.gyp` file to trigger code execution during `npm install`, bypassing most security tools that only monitor `preinstall`/`postinstall` lifecycle scripts. On developer machines it steals SSH keys, CLI credentials, browser data, and crypto wallet contents; in CI/CD environments it scrapes GitHub Actions runner memory for secrets, escalates privileges via passwordless sudo, and republishes further poisoned packages with forged SLSA provenance to continue downstream propagation.

Developers should immediately audit all direct and transitive dependencies for packages from the `@redhat-cloud-services` or Leo Platform scopes, rotate any credentials (SSH keys, API tokens, cloud access keys) present in affected build environments, and review GitHub Actions logs for unauthorized publishing events. Organizations should also add monitoring rules for `binding.gyp` execution during package installation.

### [Linux Kernel DirtyClone (CVE-2026-43503) Gets Working Public Exploit — Patch Your Kernels](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/new-dirtyclone-linux-kernel-flaw-lets.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News / JFrog Security Research | **Published:** June 25–26, 2026

JFrog Security Research published a working exploit walkthrough for CVE-2026-43503 — dubbed "DirtyClone" — on June 25, 2026. The local privilege escalation flaw (CVSS 8.8) in the Linux kernel's network packet cloning subsystem allows a local user to corrupt file-backed memory by cloning a packet through an attacker-controlled IPsec tunnel, ultimately overwriting privileged binaries such as `/usr/bin/su` to gain root access.

The root cause is that two kernel helper functions drop a critical safety flag identifying memory as shared with a file on disk when cloning a network packet, a logic gap that goes undetected by standard kernel hardening. While exploitation requires local access, many enterprise environments expose limited shell access to developers, build systems, and containers, making this a realistic privilege escalation path with significant blast radius. The kernel fix landed in mainline on May 21, 2026, but organizations running unpatched kernels remain exposed now that a public proof-of-concept exists.

All Linux distributions should apply kernel updates incorporating the May 21 mainline fix immediately. Container environments and multi-tenant systems should be treated as highest priority, and administrators should audit environments for unexpected IPsec tunnel creation by non-root users.

### [CISA Orders Immediate Patching for PTC Windchill RCE and Cisco UCM SSRF Under Active Exploitation](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/06/25/cisa-adds-two-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog)
**Source:** CISA | **Published:** June 25, 2026

CISA added two new vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a federal patching deadline of June 28, 2026. CVE-2026-12569 is a critical-severity improper input validation flaw in PTC Windchill PDMLink and PTC FlexPLM — product lifecycle management software widely used in manufacturing and defense supply chains — that allows remote code execution. CVE-2026-20230 is a server-side request forgery (SSRF) vulnerability in Cisco Unified Communications Manager (UCM), rated critical severity, that can be exploited remotely without authentication via specially crafted HTTP requests.

Both vulnerabilities have been confirmed as actively exploited in the wild. Cisco released a patch for CVE-2026-20230 on June 3, and organizations using PTC Windchill or FlexPLM should consult PTC's security advisories for available updates. FCEB agencies have until June 28 to apply fixes; all other organizations operating these products should treat the same deadline as an urgent priority.

## Threat Landscape

This week's threat landscape is defined by exploitation velocity: the Cisco SD-WAN zero-day was weaponized for months before disclosure, the Check Point VPN flaw triggered CISA's fastest-ever patching window, and a working exploit for the Linux DirtyClone flaw appeared within weeks of kernel fixes — a window that remains wide open for organizations slow to patch. Supply chain integrity is under renewed pressure, with the Miasma worm demonstrating self-propagating capability across npm ecosystems and the Tata Electronics breach showing how a single manufacturing partner compromise can expose trade secrets from multiple Fortune 500 companies. Law enforcement made a significant dent in infostealer infrastructure with Operation Endgame's seizure of 326 servers and 27 million credentials, but the rapid re-tooling of ransomware groups like World Leaks (formerly Hunter's International) and Qilin underscores that disruption rarely translates into lasting deterrence.
