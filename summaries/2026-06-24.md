### [Tata Electronics Confirms Breach: World Leaks Ransomware Dumps 200,000 Apple and Tesla Files](https://www.business-standard.com/companies/news/tata-electronics-hit-by-cyber-breach-exposing-apple-tesla-trade-secrets-126062201241_1.html)
**Source:** Business Standard / TechRadar | **Published:** June 22–23, 2026

Indian electronics manufacturing giant Tata Electronics confirmed a "cybersecurity incident" after the World Leaks ransomware group published more than 200,000 files — totaling over 630 gigabytes — on the dark web. The stolen data allegedly includes Apple manufacturing schematics, Tesla engineering documents, employee passport scans, multi-year event logs, and internal emails. Both Apple and Tesla are Tata Electronics customers, making this a significant supply-chain exposure. Apple stated it is investigating the breach.

World Leaks is widely believed to be a rebranded successor to the Hunter's International ransomware cartel, which launched in early 2025. The data has reportedly been accessible since at least June 10, 2026. Tata stated that the incident had "no impact on operations," though security researchers who reviewed the leaked files confirmed the presence of sensitive proprietary manufacturing and engineering documents.

Organizations with Tata Electronics in their supply chains should assess exposure, audit shared secrets, and verify that access controls for manufacturing data are properly segmented. The incident underscores how ransomware groups increasingly target upstream manufacturers to maximize leverage over high-profile brands.

### [Cisco SD-WAN Logs Its Seventh Zero-Day of 2026 as CISA Patch Deadline Arrives](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/cisco-catalyst-sd-wan-manager-cve-2026.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News / TechTimes | **Published:** June 23, 2026

CISA's patch deadline for CVE-2026-20245 — an actively exploited zero-day in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager — arrived on June 23, 2026, marking the seventh Cisco SD-WAN vulnerability exploited in the wild so far this year. The flaw allows an authenticated attacker with netadmin-level credentials to escalate to root by uploading a crafted file via the command-line interface, effectively granting full control over every branch site managed by the compromised controller. Affected deployments include on-premises, Cloud-Pro, Cisco Managed Cloud, and FedRAMP environments.

Cisco released a patch on June 10, 2026, and the CISA deadline was issued under Binding Operational Directive 26-04 (which superseded BOD 22-01 on the same date). While the mandate is legally binding only for Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies, CISA strongly urges all organizations to treat the flaw as high priority. Limited real-world exploitation has been observed, including incidents where unauthorized configurations were pushed to edge devices.

The repetitive nature of SD-WAN zero-days in 2026 suggests systemic weaknesses in overlapping SD-WAN codebase components. Organizations running Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN should apply the June 10 patch immediately, audit administrative credentials for any signs of compromise, and review edge device configurations for unauthorized changes.

### [ShapedPlugin WordPress Pro Plugins Backdoored in Supply Chain Attack (CVE-2026-10735)](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/shapedplugin-wordpress-pro-plugins.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News / BleepingComputer | **Published:** June 22, 2026

A sophisticated supply chain attack compromised the build and distribution pipeline of ShapedPlugin, a WordPress plugin developer with more than 400,000 active installations across its free products. Attackers breached the vendor's Easy Digital Downloads (EDD) infrastructure around May 21, 2026, and injected backdoor code into Pro plugin releases distributed via the official licensed update channel at account.shapedplugin.com. Free versions on WordPress.org were not affected.

The incident was disclosed by Wordfence Threat Intelligence on June 11 after being notified of suspicious activity. Affected products include Smart Post Show Pro (versions prior to 4.0.2), Product Slider for WooCommerce Pro (versions prior to 3.5.4), and Real Testimonials Pro (versions prior to 3.2.5). CVE-2026-10735 carries a CVSS score of 9.8; the specific WooCommerce plugin was separately assigned CVE-2026-49777 with a maximum CVSS score of 10.0. The malware registered a REST API backdoor accepting arbitrary file writes, bundled Tiny File Manager and Adminer for direct file/database GUI access, installed a webshell accepting URL-parameter commands, and included a hardcoded login bypass allowing the attacker to authenticate as any administrator without knowing their password. It also stole administrator credentials and 2FA secrets.

Any administrator who installed ShapedPlugin premium plugins between April and June 2026 should assume a potential compromise: scan for malware, remove unauthorized accounts, regenerate all 2FA secrets, and rotate all database and admin credentials immediately.

### [FortiBleed: 86,644 Fortinet Firewall Credentials Leaked in Ongoing Global Campaign](https://arcticwolf.com/resources/blog/active-fortibleed-campaign-impacting-fortinet-devices-across-194-countries/)
**Source:** Arctic Wolf / SOCRadar / CISA | **Published:** June 18–23, 2026

A large-scale automated campaign dubbed "FortiBleed" has been systematically targeting Fortinet FortiGate firewalls and SSL VPN gateways since at least February 2026, producing a verified database of over 86,644 working credential sets across 194 countries — estimated to represent roughly half of all internet-facing Fortinet devices. The campaign is attributed to a Russian-speaking threat actor and operates through full automation: attackers scan for exposed devices, test curated password lists, log every successful login, and then use compromised firewalls as listening posts to harvest additional SSL VPN credentials from passing traffic, which are fed back into the scanner.

CISA issued an alert on June 18, 2026, urging affected organizations to immediately terminate all active SSL VPN and administrative sessions, reset all Fortinet VPN and administrative passwords (especially on internet-facing systems), and enforce strong password policies. The geographic distribution of victims skews heavily toward Asia-Pacific and Europe, with Korea, China, Sweden, Malaysia, and Singapore among the most impacted countries.

Organizations running FortiGate appliances should treat any device that has been internet-facing since February as potentially compromised. The attacker's use of harvested credentials for lateral movement into managed networks makes this breach far more dangerous than a simple credential dump. Immediate password rotation, session termination, and review of firewall configurations for unauthorized changes are critical steps.

### [Squidbleed: 29-Year-Old Vulnerability in Squid Proxy Leaks Cleartext Credentials (CVE-2026-47729)](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/29-year-old-squid-proxy-bug-squidbleed.html)
**Source:** The Hacker News / SecurityWeek | **Published:** June 22–23, 2026

Security researchers have disclosed "Squidbleed" (CVE-2026-47729), a Heartbleed-style heap buffer overread vulnerability present in every version of Squid Proxy since its inception in 1997. The flaw resides in Squid's FTP gateway: by controlling an FTP server reachable from the proxy, an attacker already permitted to use the proxy can trigger a heap overread that leaks raw memory — potentially exposing other users' cleartext HTTP Authorization headers, cookies, API keys, and session tokens.

FTP support is enabled by default in Squid, and port 21 is included in the default Safe_ports ACL, making the attack trivially accessible in standard deployments. The vulnerability affects shared proxy environments such as schools, corporate offices, and public Wi-Fi hotspots. Standard HTTPS traffic riding a CONNECT tunnel is not exposed, as Squid cannot inspect inside it; only cleartext HTTP and TLS-terminating setups are at risk. The Squid maintainer has clarified that version 7.6 did not ship the actual fix, and the patch is scheduled for Squid 7.7.

Until the patch is available, the simplest mitigation is to disable FTP support entirely — a low-impact change given that FTP traffic is now nearly negligible on most networks. Organizations running Squid in multi-tenant or shared environments should treat this as urgent, given the potential for cross-user credential leakage.

## Threat Landscape

The dominant theme this week is supply chain and perimeter compromise at scale: FortiBleed demonstrates how automated credential harvesting can silently undermine tens of thousands of enterprise firewalls, while the ShapedPlugin attack shows that even the software update channel itself can no longer be trusted. Ransomware actors — particularly World Leaks, ShadowByt3$, and ShinyHunters — continue to shift toward high-leverage targets including electronics manufacturers, gaming giants, and educational institutions, with the goal of maximizing downstream exposure across household brands. Legacy infrastructure remains a critical weak point, evidenced by AryStinger exploiting decade-old router vulnerabilities and Squidbleed exposing a 29-year-old code path still active in modern deployments; the consistent message from these disclosures is that deferred patching and end-of-life hardware are actively being weaponized.
